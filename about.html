Who are you?

My name is Emily Abrash, and I'm a programmer-in-training. Currently, I'm a student in cohort 6 (C6) at Ada Developers Academy, a program in Seattle that helps women and people of non-binary gender transition to careers in web development.

I am very proud to be an Adie, and if you are considering Ada or just a similar type of career change, I would be more than happy to talk with you and help you!

What did you do before Ada?

In my "past life," I was a molecular biologist. I studied plant molecular genetics at Stanford (in the awesome Bergmann lab) and got my Ph.D. in 2015.

My thesis work focused on stomata, pores in leaves that let plants take in carbon dioxide and release oxygen and water vapor. Along the way, I did some computational modeling, timelapse imaging, and image processing, as well as lots of plain ol' molecular biology.

What areas of programming most interest you?

My current areas of interest include backend web development, graphics programming, and data science. I am especially fascinated by making pictures draw on screens, and I've been using books, tutorials, and kindly family members to self-teach C++ and SDL.

Languages: Java, C++, Python, Ruby

What do you do when you're not coding?

I love exploring shorelines, tidepools, and tidal zones, and I'm happiest when I'm within sight of the ocean.

I also enjoy reading, writing, researching obscure family history, and spending quality time with my partner and my fuzzy cat. Occasionally, I like to bake brownies.

How can I contact you?

Great question! The best way to get in touch is by email or on LinkedIn.

Please feel free to contact me if you are interested in potentially working with me, or if there is any way I can help or mentor you on your tech journey.
